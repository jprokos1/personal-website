01. About

I'm a security researcher and engineer focused on offensive tooling, adversarial machine learning, and binary vulnerability analysis. I hold a B.S. in Computer Science and an M.S. in Security Informatics (Masson Fellowship) from Johns Hopkins University, where I conducted peer-reviewed research published at USENIX Security '23.

At Two Six Technologies, I lead DARPA research programs as Principal Investigator, set technical direction across ML and binary reverse engineering domains, and work directly with government customers to translate high-level requirements into executable research. I manage direct reports and have grown from individual contributor to program lead across three roles at the same organization.

I'm drawn to problems at the edge of what's known — particularly where AI capabilities and security intersect, and where the adversarial angle hasn't been fully mapped.

03. Research

USENIX Security '23 · First Author

Squint Hard Enough: Attacking Perceptual Hashing with Adversarial Machine Learning

Jonathan Prokos, Tushar M. Jois, Neil Fendley, Roei Schuster, Matthew Green, Eran Tromer, Yinzhi Cao

We analyze the security of locality-sensitive image hashing algorithms — including PhotoDNA and PDQ — against adversarial machine learning attacks. Our work demonstrates fundamental weaknesses in perceptual hashing as a content moderation primitive, develops formal threat models for their evaluation, and introduces novel attack techniques that successfully break these systems across multiple modalities.
